About This Project

An industry-grade AI recommendation engine, designed and developed to modernize how readers discover books.

The Problem Statement

Our partner library approached us with a challenge: readers were struggling to discover new books that matched their interests. With a vast and growing collection, manual curation was no longer scalable. They needed an intelligent, automated system that could analyze reader preferences and deliver personalized recommendations in real-time.

Popularity Based Engine

The first layer of our system uses a statistical aggregation model. We process anonymized borrowing and rating data provided by the library to compute weighted engagement scores. Books that consistently receive high ratings across a diverse set of readers are surfaced as universally recommended titles. A keyword-based categorization algorithm further organizes these titles into browsable genres.

Collaborative Filtering Engine

The core ML layer implements Item-Based Collaborative Filtering using Scikit-Learn's NearestNeighbors algorithm. We construct a sparse user-book interaction matrix and compute cosine similarity distances across the entire feature space. When a reader searches for a specific title, the model instantly retrieves the most mathematically similar books based on shared reader affinity patterns — not content metadata, but actual behavioral data.

AI Chatbot Integration

To enhance the reader experience beyond simple recommendations, we integrated a conversational AI agent powered by the Llama 3.3 70B model via the Groq API. This intelligent chatbot can summarize books, answer questions about plots and themes, and provide contextual insights — essentially serving as a digital librarian available around the clock.
